ONE, TWO, A MILLION

Logline

A young man gradually falls into a thought spiral as he tries to find a logical explanation for his Sunday blues, bouncing between his physical need to feel better, and a desire for higher meaning.

Synopsis

Charlie spends the final hours of his Sunday eating ice cream alone in his flat, while a film that he has long stopped watching continues to play on his laptop. He is searching for a reason for his inexplicable melancholy when he remembers an old dream in which he was beating his mother. Doubt begins to creep in about how much control he really has over himself. His mind begins to question the authority it holds over his body. He tries to dig deeper but the deepest he can reach is his naked flesh. In the meantime, the movie on his laptop continues to play: a mythical hero from five thousand years ago is locked in a struggle not unlike his own. The next morning, Charlie wakes up fine. Yesterday feels distant, almost irrelevant. He gets dressed and leaves for work. But something has been left ajar.

Director’s Bio

Antonis Sigalas, born and raised in Athens, Greece makes his directorial debut with "One, Two, a Million". His background spans engineering, consulting, finance and business. He currently works as a Media Investment Banker at Moelis, advising on transactions across the global media and entertainment sector. Antonis has long aspired to create cinema that explores the fundamental, universal aspects of the human condition. With "One, Two a Million" on its festival run, his focus on creating independent films remains unfazed, with development already underway for his second project "Kastelorizo", a hybrid film blending fiction and documentary elements to explore the role of art in human existence. Antonis holds a degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the National Technical University of Athens and an MSc in International Finance from HEC Paris.
